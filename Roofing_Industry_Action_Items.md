## ðŸ’¡ Key Insights to Remember

### Industry Trends
- **Market shift:** New construction â†’ asset life extension & preventative maintenance
- **Post-COVID impact:** Office/retail dying, malls closing or converting to condos
- **Current drivers:** Federal money for data centers, warehouses, energy projects
- **Architectural Building Index:** Bouncing between 46-51 (under 50 = market shrinkage)
- **Future outlook:** Likely downturn, focus will shift even more to extending asset life

### Technology Gap
- **Data organization:** Most companies track at work order level, NOT deficiency level
- **Huge opportunity:** Deficiency-level tracking enables predictive analytics
- **Terry's innovation (20 years ago):** "What's failing" + "Why it's failing" = predictability
- **Still not adopted:** Most of industry still doesn't do this
- **AI potential:** Can now automate the analysis and predictions Terry was doing manually

### Sales Approach (from Tom Persia/Terry)
> "You're not selling AI, you're selling solutions. To sell solutions, you need to do a deep dive into the industry so you understand their business and can solve their actual problems."

### Roofing Contractor Challenges
- Most are NOT technology-oriented
- Rely on personal opinion as "gold standard"
- Ego: "I've installed millions of square feet, I know when a roof will fail"
- Relationship-driven: "We build personal relationships, not data relationships"
- **Exception companies** (more tech-forward):
  - Kelly Roofing
  - Advanced Roofing (Rob Canerys & sons Kevin and Mike)
  - Best Roofing (Greg Wallock)
  - Kelly Roofing

### Software Vendor Landscape
- Most vendors focus on serving contractors (1 level down)
- Should focus on contractor's customers (2 levels down)
- Opportunity to differentiate by understanding end-customer needs
